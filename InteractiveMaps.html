GIS Journey





   This webpage was created to experiment with D3 and its ability to display coordinated maps and charts that are reexpressed based on a user's input to reveal new insights, and patterns.

  This webpage was pretty stressful to make. I struggled for over a week and got frustrated quite a bit. I actually almost gave up tryingn to make this webpage but somehow was able to finally string it together. I really enjoyed how it turned out and due to the amount of time I put into this webpage would probably not change anything, simple because it represents overcoming challenges to me but it did teach me that while D3 has some great capabilities, it's not for the faint of heart unless you have a pretty good javascript foundation already.



   The goal of this map was to explore MapBox Studio by making a themed map for a fictional movie. I chose to make a vintage map similar to what you would see in a Dracula movie and aimed to depict bleeding colors of the map.

  Honestly, I enjoy this map a lot. I think it's great and sincerely wouldn't change anything about it. It was pretty difficult to find the right colors for the water with the right shades to still show the bathmetry but the work I put into this map, perfecting fonts and colors really payed off in the end and made me a huge fan of mapbox studio.



   In order to explore Leaflet and some of it's abilities, I created this map which depicts spatiotemporal information concerning the Metro Atlanta region which allows users to explore the populations of surrounding counties from the year 2000 to 2019 through proportional bubbles.

  Being the first time I used leaflet, this map was pretty difficult to make but turned out a success. If I had to change anything, it would be putting more work into the webpage itself.
