GIS Journey





   This map challenged me to work with both raster and vector data at the same time while also learning how to represent topographic data. To do this, I made a roughly 21000-mile bike tour similar to the Tour De France.

  This map was very challenging and I learning taught me the importance of carefully picking landsat data. My intial version of this map was very pixelated due to bad landsat area and I ended up having to chose new landsat data and starting almost from scratch. While this version is much better and I really enjoy that some of the labels were duplicated to create shadows of themselves so that they stand out, in retrospect I didn't think I choose the best colors for this map, everything is a bit visually loud for me.



   This infographic showcases my Adobe Illustrator skills by telling an engaging story about the decline of fertility rates while featuring a bivariate map and a graph.

  This infographic took quite a bit of time as I had to play with graphics, colors, and fonts a lot. I even tried to tell the story in an even more brief way by highlight all of the most imporant text in red. Overall I think it's really good. If I had to change something however, I think I could have worked a bit more on eliminating blank space and using more graphics in place of words.
